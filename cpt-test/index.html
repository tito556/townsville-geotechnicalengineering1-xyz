CPT Testing in Townsville: Reliable Cone Penetration Data for North Queensland Soils

Technical studies that support your project.

LEARN MORE

The weathered granite and metasediments underlying Townsville, coupled with deep alluvial deposits from the Ross and Bohle River floodplains, create a subsurface profile that often catches out designers unfamiliar with North Queensland geology. In many areas around Douglas and Idalia, clay-rich residual soils overlie rock at variable depths, while coastal suburbs near Pallarenda sit on loose sands that can exceed 10 metres in thickness. A CPT test provides a continuous, near-real-time record of tip resistance and sleeve friction without the sample disturbance that can compromise borehole-derived parameters. For sites where the groundwater table sits within two metres of the surface—something we routinely encounter in Townsville’s wet season—the pore pressure measurements from a piezocone unit become essential for quantifying drainage conditions during loading.

In Townsville's variable alluvial and residual profiles, a CPT log typically delivers more stratigraphic detail per metre than any other single field investigation method.

Methodology and scope

Under AS 1726-2017, the cone penetration test must follow procedures that produce repeatable, high-resolution profiles suitable for direct geotechnical input. In Townsville’s reactive clay profiles, the friction ratio from a CPT log often reveals the transition zone between residual soil and weathered rock more clearly than SPT blow counts can resolve. This matters when you are designing deep footings or assessing settlement in compressible layers. Our approach integrates cone data with grain size analysis on thin-walled tube samples taken from critical horizons, because while the CPT can identify a change in soil behaviour type, linking those findings to a physical description of particle distribution adds confidence to the engineering model. The Ross River floodplain, for instance, frequently contains interbedded silts and fine sands that the piezocone discriminates with high reliability when pore pressure dissipation tests are included in the programme.

Local considerations

The most common mistake we see in Townsville is relying solely on SPT correlations for pile design in the floodplain, then discovering during construction that refusal depths vary by more than three metres across a single building footprint. Because the cone provides a continuous trace, it catches thin, stiff layers that a 1.5-metre SPT interval can easily miss. In slope stability assessments around Castle Hill’s steep terrain, overlooking the low undrained shear strength of saturated residual clays—directly measurable via CPT pore pressure response—has led to costly remedial works after the first significant wet season. A focused CPT campaign, even with just four or five soundings, often prevents these surprises by mapping the stratigraphic variability that defines geotechnical risk in coastal North Queensland.

Frequently asked questions

How much does CPT testing cost for a residential block in Townsville?

For a standard single-storey house site in Townsville, a CPT investigation with two soundings to 10–15 metres depth typically falls between AU$250 and AU$430 per sounding, inclusive of setup, mobilisation, and an AS 1726-compliant report. The final cost depends on access conditions, the number of dissipation tests requested, and whether pre-drilling is needed to penetrate any shallow cemented layers.

Can CPT replace boreholes entirely for a Townsville site investigation?

In many of the alluvial and coastal sand profiles we test across Townsville, CPT data provides higher stratigraphic resolution than conventional boreholes and can reduce the number of SPT holes required. However, AS 1726 still calls for physical sampling at a minimum number of locations to confirm soil type and allow laboratory testing. We usually recommend a hybrid programme: CPT at most grid points with one or two boreholes for sampling and Atterberg limits, which gives both the continuous profile and the material verification the standard expects.

How long does a typical CPT investigation take in Townsville?

A single CPT sounding to 15 metres, including rig setup and pore pressure dissipation pauses, usually takes between 45 and 90 minutes in the alluvial profiles common around the Ross River. A full residential investigation with two or three soundings is normally completed within half a day, with the preliminary log available immediately on site and the final interpreted report delivered within three working days.
