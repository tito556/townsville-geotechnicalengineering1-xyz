Process overview

A common mistake we see on Townsville projects is assuming that all sand units below the water table are liquefiable. That shortcut leads to expensive over-design of Improvement when a targeted soil liquefaction analysis would have excluded the denser layers. Our workflow starts with high-quality sampling: thin-walled tube recovery in silty sands, split-spoon in granular zones. We then run cyclic triaxial or cyclic simple shear on selected specimens to calibrate the site-specific CRR curve. The triaxial data feeds directly into the NCEER framework, replacing conservative empirical defaults. For sites where penetration resistance is borderline, we often recommend supplementary CPT testing to get a continuous profile of Ic and qt, which resolves thin interbedded layers that standard SPT intervals miss.

Local context

On the flat ground south of the Ross River, we've repeatedly logged loose fine sand from 2 to 5 metres depth, fully saturated during the wet season. That unit is exactly where a soil liquefaction analysis in Townsville predicts triggering at PGA values below the 500-year design event. Lateral spreading toward creek channels is the real failure mode here, not bearing capacity loss. The ground heave and vertical settlement after shaking can rupture services and crack slab-on-grade footings. We report settlements layer by layer using the Ishihara-Yoshimine procedure, giving the structural engineer a firm number for differential movement. For earth-retaining structures, the post-liquefaction lateral pressure coefficient often governs the design—something a standard retaining walls static check will miss.

Top questions

What is the typical cost of a soil liquefaction analysis for a Townsville site?

For a single-structure site with one SPT borehole and lab index testing, the analysis typically runs between AU$3,950 and AU$5,760. The final cost depends on depth to rock, number of samples requiring cyclic triaxial testing, and whether CPT is added.

Which AS 1726 classification data do you need to run the NCEER method?

We need the corrected SPT N60 or CPT tip resistance qt and friction ratio, soil description (group symbol and fines content), unit weight or density index, and the groundwater level measured at the time of drilling. All of this comes from a standard AS 1726 field log.

How do you account for Townsville's seasonal groundwater fluctuation in the analysis?

We run sensitivity cases at both the measured wet-season groundwater level and a conservative long-term high level derived from local monitoring records. The cyclic resistance ratio is corrected for the effective overburden stress at each scenario, and the final report presents results for the governing case.
