Electrical Resistivity Surveys & Vertical Electrical Sounding in Townsville

Technical studies that support your project.

LEARN MORE

Townsville's subsurface varies sharply between the heavy clays along Ross River floodplain and the weathered granite profiles up on Castle Hill. On the flats you might hit saline groundwater at less than 3 metres; on the slopes, fresh granodiorite can appear at 6 or 8 metres. That contrast demands a non-invasive method that draws a clear line between conductive clays and resistive rock. Electrical resistivity and Vertical Electrical Sounding do exactly that. We run Schlumberger and Wenner arrays to map stratigraphy before a single bucket goes in the ground. For projects where the target is deeper than 20 metres, VES gives a continuous resistivity log that helps decide whether you need a pile foundation or if a mat foundation will handle differential settlement. Townsville's monsoonal wet season saturates the upper layers fast, and resistivity contrasts shift with moisture content, so timing the survey right matters as much as the electrode spacing.

A resistivity contrast of 5:1 between alluvium and bedrock is our typical trigger for a sharp layer boundary in Townsville's coastal plain.

Methodology and scope

Townsville sits on a complex mix of Quaternary alluvium, decomposed granite, and coastal estuarine sediments. The unconfined aquifer beneath the Burdekin delta fringe can show resistivity values below 10 ohm-m where saltwater intrusion is present, while dry residual soils above the weathering front often exceed 200 ohm-m. Our field crew uses a 48-electrode system with dipole-dipole and gradient arrays for 2D imaging, and VES with AB/2 spacings up to 150 metres for deeper profiling. Every sounding ties back to NATA-accredited laboratory measurements of pore-water conductivity and moisture content on recovered samples. The data feeds directly into AS 1726 site classification, and when the profile flags low-resistivity zones, we correlate with in-situ permeability testing to confirm hydraulic connectivity. We deliver colour contoured pseudo-sections alongside tabulated layer resistivities and interpreted lithology columns, ready for your geotechnical engineer to plug into the ground model.

Local considerations

Townsville's elevation averages just 6 metres above sea level across much of the urban area, and the city sits in a Wind Region C cyclonic zone under AS/NZS 1170. Shallow saline groundwater corrodes buried concrete and steel, and undetected paleochannels filled with soft organic silts can cause differential settlement that cracks slabs within the first two wet seasons. We have seen VES profiles where a 2-metre-thick low-resistivity lens at 4 metres depth was the only signature of a buried mangrove deposit, completely invisible from the surface. Skipping resistivity before excavation in these conditions means foundation concrete goes into aggressive soil without adequate sulphate-resisting cement, or a dewatering system gets sized for clean sand when it should handle silt. The electrical method cuts through that uncertainty: it tells you where the salt is, where the rock is, and where the soft spots hide, all without drilling a hundred boreholes.

Frequently asked questions

How deep can a VES survey see in Townsville soils?

With an AB/2 spacing of 150 metres we typically resolve layers down to 50 or 60 metres. The actual depth depends on the resistivity contrast between layers: the sharper the jump from conductive clay to resistive rock, the clearer the boundary. In the Ross River floodplain where clays dominate, a 50-metre sounding is usually sufficient to reach fresh granite or metasediment.

What does a VES survey cost for a standard residential block?

For a typical residential lot in Townsville, a VES sounding with 3 to 4 depth points runs between AU$870 and AU$1.570, depending on access conditions and the number of soundings required. A 2D ERT line along a single alignment is at the upper end of that range and includes the full processed cross-section.

How does the wet season affect resistivity readings?

Heavy monsoonal rain saturates the top 2 to 3 metres of soil, lowering near-surface resistivity significantly. We account for this by measuring pore-water conductivity on recovered samples and applying moisture corrections during inversion. For critical projects we recommend running the survey during the dry season, May to October, when the water table is stable and contrasts are sharpest.

Can resistivity tell me if my soil is corrosive to concrete?

Yes. Resistivity below 20 ohm-m generally indicates aggressive conditions where sulphate attack or chloride-induced corrosion of reinforcement is a risk. We combine the field resistivity with laboratory pH, sulphate content, and chloride levels to give you an AS 1726 soil aggressivity classification so your structural engineer can specify the right exposure class and cover.

Do I need boreholes if I have an ERT profile?

An ERT profile tells you where the boundaries are and what the bulk resistivity looks like, but it does not replace boreholes for strength parameters. The best approach is a pair of calibration boreholes at key resistivity transitions: one in the conductive zone and one in the resistive zone. We use the borehole logs to convert resistivity values to lithology, and then the ERT fills in the gaps between boreholes with continuous coverage.
